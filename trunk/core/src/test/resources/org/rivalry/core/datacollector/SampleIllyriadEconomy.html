The Royal Institute of Illyrian Architects (RIIA) has announced the winners of their most recent design competition. The competition was introduced to showcase and promote the very best in new architecture that each of Illyria's races were able to produce.

Mercalo Brougham, Chairman of the RIIA, said "We examined all entrants for not only the beauty of their form but also the utility of their function, and we believe that our chosen winners reflect the best that Illyrian architecture has to offer. We're delighted by the quality of the entrants and winners.

The aesthetics of each architect reflects some of their races' character. For example, structures such as the Saboteur's Sanctuary are obviously of Orcish design; whereas the Mana Projector could only be the work of Elves."

He continued, "So that all may benefit from these new buildings we are releasing construction blueprints widely available to any who learns the necessary skills. Plans for sixteen of the new buildings have already been released , and we're just finishing copying the remaining four blueprints now, to be made available over the next few days."

The competition was not without its detractors, however. In a widely publicised spat, the Illyria Trade Council withdrew its sponsorship of the RIIA event because of the lack of focus on any building designs that had trade-specific functions.

Trademaster Raffine commmented: "It's all well and good building dozens of ethereal Elven buildings, if you like that sort of thing... but where's the profit in that? We originally chose to sponsor this competition because we were assured we'd be getting something out of it - something a darn sight more useful than arches, spires and spindles that do nothing more than 'soar gracefully'.

So they didn't get our sponsorship money, and good riddance to them. We've now got our own team of good, solid architects working on some designs that will be actually useful to the Illyria Trade Council . The RIIA should remember that trade makes money, and money is what pays for all this... this... frippery."

As for the RIIA's next project, we asked Mercalo Brougham to comment on rumours that they were working on new plans on behalf of the Circle of Five , but he refused to comment, saying emphatically "It's not worth losing my life in unspeakably horrific ways, simply to speak to The Herald. Sorry."

Geomancer Herrolf of the Circle of Five has made a remarkable discovery of great import to the use and practice of Magick in Illyria.

"We know from the various Histories that Elementals have existed throughout the land of Illyria, but this is the first time we have been able to predict how and when they arise." he said.

"The energies released by lifeforce blend with the natural elements as well as the Spirits of the land - of Nature itself - and can cause Elementals to arise, bound to serve the first being that they encounter."

With a wry smile he added, "Obviously, the amount of lifeforce that blends with the land is critical; and the ancient Tournament battle sites around the Kingdom have witnessed the largest shedding of blood in recent years, so it's no surprise that Elementals are now rising at these locations! There have also been unconfirmed reports of Elementals rising at dead Cities as well, apparently drawn by the death that has occurred at these abandoned husks."

Whilst Geomancer Herrolf does not believe that the precise time when these Elementals rise is an exact science, he says that "Based on current observations, we're getting around four sightings every day on each Tournament Battle site. The first person the Elemental encounters in occupation of one of these sites when it rises, this person will bind the Elemental to his or her town."

The Council of Illyria has expressed interest in discovering more about these creatures, and will additionally reward players who bind Elementals to them with 20 Prestige per Elemental bound, to be distributed at midday on the 15th of July .

The Council added "But be warned, only one Lord or Lady can be in posession of the territory at the time the Elemental rises - and for this reason all Alliance, NAP and Confederation rules are suspended at these locations for the duration. Each Lord or Lady is on his or her own for this one."


Historians from the Order of Allembine are seeking to clarify several distant details in the family tree of our beloved King Sigurd.

At the foundation of the current dynasty, some 800 years ago, it is known that statues were erected across Illyria to the first King’s two Grandfathers and 18 Great Uncles, all of whom carried arms in the struggle to establish the dynasty. However, the Order of Allembine at that stage had more urgent duties than the recording of the Royal Family’s predecessors, and so no written records were made.

The Order now invites all lords and ladies holding settlements in Illyria to assist with their research. Rulers wishing to assist might start by considering the statues of the King’s antecedents, which can be seen scattered about the lands.

The Order are hoping to confirm, from multiple sources, the names and any life details of these 20 royal forbears.

The Order seeks evidence and worn testimony, not rumour and hearsay, so rulers must provide both the names and sworn verbal testimony as to how the names were gathered – for example, the Order will want to know that a settlement’s diplomats have personally visited a site which offers evidence; it is not enough to have heard a rumour from another ruler or another settlement.

Bowing to pressure from the King's brother, Lord Grendam, the Council of Illyria today announced a radical increase to the cost of an Alliance Charter.

Alliances could previously be established for as little as 5,000 Gold by Lords who had advanced their diplomatic skills. The cost of establishing a new Alliance has been increased one hundred-fold , and new Alliance Charters will only be issued to Lords in return for 500,000 Gold Pieces .

A lively debate was held in Chambers at Centrum with Trademaster Raffine decrying the move, saying in an impassioned speech that "... The price of a Charter has been long-established by the King's own great-grandfather - and this increase is a wanton act of greed so venal that even the Illyria Trade Council would balk at it!"

Envoy Karlygon, Grantsman of Charters on behalf of the Council, defended the rise with equal passion.

"It is clear to all of us with eyes to see that the continued banding together of Lords, operating outside the wise rules and established covenants of His Majesty King Sigurd, is a threat to us all. We're not abolishing Alliances - as many of them undoubtedly have their uses - we're simply ensuring that the coffers of the Council do not suffer unnecessarily in return for this increased assumption of risk."

He added, "Has it also escaped the Trade Council's notice that there are additional threats abroad in the land, and that even today, wandering Trolls threaten some of their own lakeside cities, such as Shelton ? We need funds to combat these threats."

Speaking after the new Law had been enacted, Trademaster Raffine cast aspersions on all that had been said in the Chamber. After dismissing the threat from the Trolls as "negligible" he questioned the timing of this hike, asking "Why now? And why so much? What does the Council really intend to do with these riches? What aren't they telling us?"


His Majesty King Sigurd of the Council of Illyria is pleased to announce a One Month Tournament starting immediately, with the main aim of improving our knowledge of the World.

A new Museum of Natural History has been built at Centrum and has been staffed by biologists and naturologists from the Empirium. The only thing they lack are a wide variety of specimens from the various creatures that inhabit the lands of Illyria.

His Majesty bids all the Lords of Illyria to send their armies far and wide to slay these creatures . When your army slays each group of beasts there is a chance that your troops can retrieve prime anatomical specimens. The more beasts that are present at a location, the more likelihood that usable remains can be retrieved.

Your army will return to your home town as usual, carrying the specimens and these will be visible in your town's Inventory . The specimens cannot be traded or otherwise transported away from a town at this time, as such an endeavour might damage them and render them useless to the Museum!

At Midday on Wednesday, May 11th the tournament will end, and highly-trained transporters from the Illyria Trade Council will collect all those specimens that are held in each Lord's town.

As this is a mighty work of knowledge to benefit the Kingdom as a whole, His Majesty has forbidden the theft of these specimens from player towns for the duration of the tournament - although his military advisors suggest that Lords might certainly use some more cunning and underhand tactics to hamper their competitors' efforts.

There will, of course, be prizes for winning the tournaments' various categories, and these prizes are designed to encourage individual Lords as well as Alliance competition.

Details on the prize categories, the prizes themselves and the rules are available here . The currently leading Lords and Alliances in each category are available on the Tournaments page.

His Majesty eagerly anticipates these specimens and the results of the valuable research into their possible uses, and bids every Lord and Alliance the best of luck in this endeavour.
